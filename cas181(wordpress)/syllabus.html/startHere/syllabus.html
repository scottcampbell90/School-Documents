Course Description

Introduces the creation of sophisticated, dynamic, interactive and fully functional websites using WordPress, a Content Management System (CMS). Includes installing and modifying templates, creating efficient site navigation using menus, organizing a site using components, enhancing a site with plugins, creating a blog, and creating user functionality with user logins.

Recommended: CAS 111D, CAS 206, and CAS 215; placement into RD 115 and WR 115. Audit available.

Instructional Approach

CAS 181W is a beginning class for learning WordPress. This course is arranged chronologically and employs a modular design. Each week, students complete all assignments listed in that week's module. All modules appear in the Content area during the entire course.

Due dates for each week's assignments will be listed on the Course Calendar which is available in the Content area. Assignments within the modules may direct students to use the tools contained in Desire2Learn, including the Dropbox and Discussions. These tools will be accessible both from within the weekly content modules as well as from the course navigation bar.

The Learning modules are listed for each week in the term. When you open the table of contents for each week, you will see a lecture page and an assignment page. Each week's module has assignments from the Knitting website and the Landscaping website. If there is a quiz for that module, the quiz will be listed as well. Discussion questions will appear throughout the course and will have links in the Modules too.

Communication Guidelines

Communicate by using the Desire2Learn e-mail system which can be accessed from the course navigation. You can use Desire2Learn e-mail to send a private e-mail to me and/or classmates, which is located in the top right corner of the window.

If your question or comment would be of interest to other students, please post it to the Discussion area for that module. This way other participants can help answer questions, and all participants will benefit from the answers. Please refer to the information on "netiquette" in the introductory module for guidelines governing the content of written communications. Your first communication assignment is to introduce yourself in the discussion topic "Introductions."

Learning Outcomes

  • Use WordPress software to create personal and/or business websites following current professional and/or industry standards.
  • Use critical thinking skills to design and create dynamic WordPress websites.

Activities, Assignments and Assessments

Assignments

Your understanding of the concepts and your ability to work in WordPress will be assessed by the completion of your assignments and an individual project. All assignments must be completed to successfully finish the class. Late assignments will not be accepted with the exception of one "free" late assignment. See the Late Policy for more information on this.

Module Assignments  - Each week you'll work through assignments in the module. One will be from the Yarn Over yarn shop and the other will be from PCC Landscaping, two fictitious sites that were created for this course.

Project  - Your individual project is to design, develop, and publish a website using recommended design practices. Your website will contain a home page and at least four (but no more than 6) pages. You will have two project update deadlines during the term and the project is due during Finals Week. Learn more about the Project.

Discussion Questions  - There will be discussion questions that you are required to respond to during the course. Links to the discussion questions will be appear in the modules in which they are assigned.

Grades are based on your hands-on assignments, Project, Discussion Questions and Quizzes. Feedback will be given on assignments and grades will be posted within one week after the due date. Be sure to look at the Grades after they have been graded in order to receive feedback.

Academic Integrity (rules about cheating, plagiarism, or sharing work)

Cheating is against PCC policy. Cheating includes any attempt to defraud, deceive, or mislead the instructor in arriving at an honest grade assessment, and may include copying answers from other students or using unauthorized notes during tests. Plagiarism is a particular form of cheating that involves presenting as one's own the ideas or work of another, and may include using other people's ideas without proper attribution and submitting another person's work as one's own. Violations of the cheating policy will result in a grade of '0' for the assignment in question, and may result in a failing grade for the course at the instructor's discretion.
